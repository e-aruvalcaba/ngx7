Typography

The consistent use of the Roboto family will ensure that every touch point and medium communicates its message effectively through a common language. Roboto has great variety in weights and can be implemented in different combinations based on the type of message, the appropriate hierarchy and/or the desired cadence to engage the intended audience.

Font weights should be determined based on whether the message is a heading, body, navigation, footer, link, etc.

Each will require a different treatment, and the right application will ensure readability and sound hierarchy.

Price style

Prices and monetary values should be displayed consistently across the entire application.

There should always be two decimal places displayed after the dollar amount.

No subscript or superscript should be used when displaying money.

As seen in the examples to the right, the currency symbol will appear in the same size type as the value either directally prior to or after.

Date and Time Format

The presentation of date and time should be consistent throughout the user experience. It is important to consider a users geographical region when presenting date + time information. For this reason, please use the standard format by location.

US

12-hour digital clocks, with a.m. and p.m. Example: 11:00 a.m. - 2:00 p.m.

Date format: numeric date “MM/DD/YYYY” and expanded form "January 1, 2017" and short form “Jan 1, 2017"

MEXICO

12-hour digital clocks, with a.m. and p.m. Example: 11:00 a.m. - 2:00 p.m.

Date format: numeric date “DD/MM/YYYY” and expanded form “1 January 2017" and short form “1 Jan 2017"

EUROPEAN

12-hour digital clocks, with a.m. and p.m. Example: 11:00 a.m. - 2:00 p.m.

Date format: numeric date “DD/MM/YYYY” and expanded form “1 January 2017" and short form “1 Jan 2017"
